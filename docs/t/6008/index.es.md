# ¿Cuáles son las empresas del Nasdaq 100?

## 1. ¿Qué es el Nasdaq 100?

El **Nasdaq 100** es un índice bursátil que agrupa a las **100 empresas no financieras más grandes** que cotizan en el mercado Nasdaq, ordenadas por capitalización bursátil.

Incluye principalmente compañías de los sectores:

- Tecnología  
- Consumo  
- Biotecnología  
- Telecomunicaciones  
- Industria  

Su composición se revisa y actualiza periódicamente.

---

## 2. ¿Por qué es importante este índice?

- Es uno de los índices más representativos de la **innovación tecnológica**.  
- Su rendimiento suele estar impulsado por algunas de las empresas más influyentes del mundo.  
- Muchos inversionistas lo utilizan como referencia para invertir en empresas de crecimiento.  
- Puede replicarse fácilmente mediante ETFs como el QQQ.

## 4. Sector Composition

The approximate composition of the Nasdaq 100 is usually distributed as follows:

* **Technology**: around 55–60%
* **Consumer Discretionary**: 15–20%
* **Healthcare and Biotechnology**: 5–7%
* **Telecommunications, Industrials, and other sectors**: the rest of the index

This shows its clear tendency toward technology and innovation-driven companies.

---

## 5. How Companies Are Selected for the Nasdaq 100

Companies must meet certain criteria:

1. Be listed on the Nasdaq market.
2. Not be a financial company.
3. Rank among the largest by market capitalization.
4. Have sufficient liquidity (high trading volume).
5. Pass periodic reviews to remain in the index.

---

## 6. How to Invest in the Nasdaq 100

You can invest in this index through:

* **ETFs**: such as QQQ or similar funds that replicate the index’s performance.
* **Individual stocks**: by directly buying shares of the companies included in the index.
* **Index funds**: designed to follow its behavior.

---

## 7. Advantages and Risks

### Advantages

* High exposure to leading technology companies.
* Long-term growth potential.
* Diversification within the innovative sector.

### Risks

* High concentration in a few technology companies.
* Higher volatility compared to more diversified indices.
* Sector-specific risk if technology enters strong corrections.

---

The Nasdaq 100 is a key index for those looking to invest in **innovative, high-growth companies**. Although it is dominated by the technology sector, it includes other important sectors such as consumer and biotechnology.

It is a useful tool both for new investors and for those seeking exposure to leading global companies, always considering the associated risks.
